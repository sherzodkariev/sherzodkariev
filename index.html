About me

Hello there! My name is Sherzod Kariev. I am a junior at the University of South Florida majoring in Computer Science. Currently, I am conducting undergraduate research in Social Computing Research Lab led by Dr. Sriram Chellappan. Research areas we are exploring include Use of Deep Learning in Identifying Species of Most Dangerous Mosquitoes and Analysis Framework for Cyber Bullying.

I am inspired in doing research, so I also work in Vision Sensor Lab led by Dmitry Goldgof and Dr. Lawrence Hall. We implement Convolutional Neural Networks for Lung Cancer Prediction and Identification of Malignant Tumor Images. I believe these are the most valuable projects I contributed to in my life, which are beneficial for people all over the world.

Previously, I had an experience of working as an Undergraduate Teaching Assistant for Discrete Structures class led by Dr. Rafael Perez, helping out students to learn concepts of Discrete Mathematics.

Other than that, I am an active member of the Society of Competitive Programmers at University of South Florida. We participate at various hackathons all over the country.
